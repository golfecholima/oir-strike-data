                     Military Airstrikes Continue Against ISIL in Syria and Iraq

SOUTHWEST ASIA ­ On Feb. 7, U.S. and Coalition military forces continued to attack
ISIL terrorists in Syria, using fighter, attack and remotely piloted aircraft to conduct 5
airstrikes. Separately, U.S. and Coalition military forces conducted 7 airstrikes in Iraq,
using fighter, attack and remotely piloted aircraft against ISIL terrorists. All strikes took
place between 8 a.m., Feb. 7, and 8 a.m., Feb. 8, local time.

         The following is a summary of the strikes conducted since the last press release:

         Syria
     Near Ar Raqqah, an airstrike struck a military garrison.
     Near Kobani, three airstrikes struck one large tactical unit, two small tactical

         units, and destroyed an ISIL vehicle.
     Near Dayr az Zawr, an airstrike struck one tactical unit and destroyed an ISIL

         guard shack.
         .

         Iraq

     Near Fallujah, three airstrikes struck two ISIL tactical units, destroyed one boat
         and one ISIL vehicle.

     Near Bayji, two airstrikes struck one large ISIL tactical unit, one small tactical unit
         and destroyed two ISIL buildings.

     Near Mosul, two airstrikes destroyed two fighting positions.

         Airstrike assessments are based on initial reports. All aircraft returned to base
safely.

         The strikes were conducted as part of Operation Inherent Resolve, the operation
to eliminate the ISIL terrorist group and the threat they pose to Iraq, Syria, the region,
